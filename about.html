About Me

Hi! My name is Grace, and I am a freelance science writer based in San Francisco, CA. When I'm not writing, I am working on my PhD in Neuroscience at Stanford University, where I use computational models to try to understand how mental illness works in the brain.

My writing focuses on the intersection of neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and society. With both scientific expertise and a passion for clear communication, I strive to make complex topics accessible without sacrificing accuracy or nuance.

In 2020, I was the AAAS Mass Media Fellow at WIRED, and I have also written for Scientific American, Slate, and Popular Science, among other outlets. Between 2016 and 2018, I studied neuroscience and gender studies at the University of Oxford.

My work explores questions like:

  • How are new technologies changing our understanding of the brain?
  • What ethical considerations arise as AI becomes more integrated into healthcare?
  • How can we make science more inclusive and equitable?
  • What can neuroscience tell us about human behavior and society?

I'm particularly interested in stories that challenge conventional wisdom, highlight underrepresented perspectives, or reveal unexpected connections between different fields of research.
